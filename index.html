Payday promotion compilation

The marketing team at Traveloka Indonesia took the initiative to consolidate all promotion pages for Traveloka products into a unified main interface. This effort aimed to help users in easily locating relevant promotions during monthly payday events and minimizing scroll time within the promotion menu.

As sole web designer & developer, I collaborated closely with the marketing project lead to deliberate on the layout.

My role
UI/UX designer
Website developer
Design QA

Context
To provide the best experience for users during the payday period, Traveloka Indonesia offers promotions across 6 products. These promotions appear as cards under the promotion menu, featuring a mix of both related and unrelated payday promotions. To prevent an overwhelming number of cards on the promotion page, the marketing team aims to consolidate all related payday promotions into a single card. This approach ensures that all promotion pages related to payday are seamlessly combined into one entrance under the promotion menu.
Problem
The existing recurring template landing page fails to meet the requirements, particularly with regard to accommodating promotions from 6 products. Additionally, uncertainties appear regarding the number of promotions that will be active during the payday period. Adding complexity, the promotions will cover both domestic and international travel.
Goal
Deliver a main entry page featuring thumbnails for promotions from each product (totaling 6 products at that time) consolidated on a single page.
Concepting
This project has several requirements to be met. Firstly, each Traveloka product will offer multiple promotions. Secondly, these promotions will encompass both domestic and international travel.

To address this needs, the initial consideration is to create a structured section, resembling a table, with each product in Y-axis and accommodate promotions for both domestic and international travel in X-axis.
Preview
Learnings
The initial design faced a setback as users mistakenly clicked on the non-clickable product box on the left, disrupted the intended flow. Consequently, users exited the page, leading to a drop in promotion sales.

This served as a valuable lesson, prompting me to recover with an iteration design for the subsequent payday promotion. The revamped design proved highly successful, remaining in use until the conclusion of the promotion payday plan.

The Traveloka Malaysia design team requires a campaign page with a "travel easy" concept to enhance user awareness of the company's product. Subsequently, following the successful launch in Malaysia, the Traveloka Philippines design team eager to implement a similar idea with a different layout.

My role

Website developer

Context

Design Information and Self-service Hub (DISH)

The Traveloka design team faced challenges in accessing essential data, as the absence of a centralized platform led to prolonged search times.

As a project driver and design operations for design team, I collaborated with my team to conduct surveys and interviews with all designers (approximately 200 in total). The result was the development of a unified platform named DISH, serving as a one-stop solution that consolidates all information required by designers.

As a webmaster, I closely collaborated with the VP of the Design team to compile essential data, providing a structured framework for designers to follow within the company.

This project significantly reduced search times for designers and gained recognition from other teams as well.

My role
Project Driver
UI/UX designer
Website developer
Webmaster

Context
At Traveloka Indonesia, each product or function has its own dedicated design team. The Design Ops team operates independently and is tasked with overseeing and fulfilling all the designers' requirements to enhance their efficiency. This project aims to serve as a guidebook, facilitating quick access to necessary information and ensuring alignment in design across different functions.
Problem
As a tech company, Traveloka Indonesia utilizes a cloud-service to store all information. With a lot of data to access, individuals often resort to saving links, such as in browser bookmarks. However, this process can be troublesome, especially considering the varied approaches each individual may take in storing the required information.
Goal
Launched a centralized page to consolidate all essential information, ensuring design alignment across diverse functions.
Concepting
The survey revealed that designers require access not only to design-related data but also to non-design-related information, such as a general details about Traveloka.

To ensure seamless transition and low learning curve, I explored into Google apps and opted for Google Sites to establish DISH. Personally designing the website architecture, I engaged in a trial-and-error process to determine the most effective components and layout offered by Google Sites.

Furthermore, all critical information is documented in a cloud-based platform, making it more convenient to use a list concept for compiling links through hyperlinked text within multiple categories.

DSGN Library Management System

The designers at Traveloka Indonesia have a physical book lending system. Unfortunately, due to some neglect in management, several books have gone missing or have been damaged. This issue is impacting the design team's budget, as replacements must be provided for any missing books.

To preserve the physical book collection, I initiated a project aimed at retrieving and maintaining both existing and new books. Collaborating closely with the VP of Design, we secured a dedicated space to house all the books, creating a functional and organized design library.

My role
Project Manager
System Architecture
Developer

Context
Many designers are keen on self-improvement through reading and applying insights to their work, prompting the establishment of the existing lending system. This system employs a self-updating status. However, there is a lack of clear regulations for designers to follow and the person in charge is not clearly defined.
Problem
The previous lending system utilized a tool with limited accessibility. This self-updating system lacked a structured return schedule, resulting in untracked physical books and fostering irresponsible behavior.
Goal
Developed a new library system with an updated lending flow designed to track the books and automatically remind the borrower to return them on time.
Concepting
To enhance accessibility, the platform was migrated to the company's general tools, specifically Google Workspace. This change ensures that all individuals within Traveloka can access the link without restrictions.

In terms of the system flow, I synchronized Google Spreadsheet, Google Calendar, and Google Form to establish an automatic system. Google Spreadsheet functions as an online library or book database, Google Form serves as the platform to submit requests to borrow available books, and Google Calendar acts as a reminder in the borrower’s calendar, indicating when the book should be returned.

The initial step to borrow a book involves submitting data through Google Form. Subsequently, users are not required to take any action in the book database, as the information from Google Form is automatically transferred to Google Spreadsheet, updating the book status in the database. Along with the process, the modified status in the book database then triggers an auto-reminder on the borrower's calendar.
